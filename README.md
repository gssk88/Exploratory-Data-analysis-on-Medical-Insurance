**Medical Insurance Cost Analysis (Exploratory Data Analysis)**

**Project Overview**

This project performs **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** on a medical insurance dataset to understand the key factors that influence **health insurance charges**.

The dataset contains demographic and health-related information such as **age, gender, BMI (Body Mass Index), number of children, smoking habits, region, and insurance charges**.

The main objective of this project is to analyze how these factors impact **medical insurance costs** and uncover patterns that could help in **risk assessment and pricing strategies in the healthcare industry**.

---

**Objectives**

* Understand the structure and characteristics of the dataset
* Perform **data cleaning and preprocessing**
* Analyze relationships between **insurance charges and demographic factors**
* Identify the **most influential variables affecting medical costs**
* Detect **outliers and unusual patterns**
* Visualize trends using **data visualization techniques**

---

**Dataset Description**

The dataset includes the following variables:

| Feature  | Description                                                     |
| -------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Age      | Age of the individual                                           |
| Sex      | Gender of the insured person                                    |
| BMI      | Body Mass Index indicating body fat level                       |
| Children | Number of dependents covered by insurance                       |
| Smoker   | Whether the individual is a smoker                              |
| Region   | Residential region (Northeast, Northwest, Southeast, Southwest) |
| Charges  | Annual medical insurance cost                                   |

**Exploratory Data Analysis**

The following analyses were conducted:

* Distribution of **insurance charges**
* Relationship between **age and medical expenses**
* Impact of **BMI on insurance charges**
* Comparison of **smokers vs non-smokers medical costs**
* Effect of **number of children on insurance charges**
* Regional comparison of healthcare expenses

---

**Key Insights**

* **Smokers pay significantly higher insurance charges** compared to non-smokers.
* **Age has a positive correlation with insurance costs**, meaning older individuals tend to pay more.
* Individuals with **higher BMI often incur higher medical expenses**.
* The number of children has **minimal impact on insurance charges**.
* Regional differences exist but are not as significant as smoking and BMI.
